Early Life and Influences: Foundations of a Future Executive

Claude Edward Elkins Jr’s upbringing laid the groundwork for his future achievements. Raised in the heart of Southwest Virginia, he was immersed in a region shaped by industrial heritage, railroads, and a culture that valued hard work and resilience. From a young age, these surroundings instilled in him a disciplined work ethic that would carry him through every phase of his career.

Before transitioning into the railroad sector, Claude Edward Elkins Jr served in the United States Marine Corps. His military service reinforced values such as accountability, discipline, and decisive leadership under pressure—qualities that later defined his executive approach in transportation and logistics.

His academic path was equally distinctive. He earned a Bachelor of Arts in English from the University of Virginia’s College at Wise, sharpening communication and analytical skills. He later completed an MBA with a focus on Port and Maritime Economics from Old Dominion University, deepening his understanding of global trade, logistics, and supply chain economics.

Starting Small: From Brakeman to Railroad Operations Expert

Claude Edward Elkins Jr began his professional railroad career in 1988 as a road brakeman for Norfolk Southern. This hands-on role required physical endurance, technical awareness, and a deep understanding of train safety and operations. Starting at ground level gave him unmatched insight into how freight rail truly functions.

Over time, he advanced through operational roles including conductor, locomotive engineer, and relief yardmaster. Each position broadened his perspective and reinforced his credibility across all levels of the workforce.

Transition to Commercial Leadership: Bridging Operations and Strategy

After mastering operational roles, Elkins transitioned into intermodal marketing, where he began connecting rail operations with trucking, shipping, and customer-driven logistics solutions. This shift marked his evolution into a leader capable of aligning real-world operations with commercial strategy.

His ability to translate customer needs into operationally realistic solutions became a defining strength, setting him apart as a leader who could balance ambition with execution.

Climbing the Corporate Ladder: Senior Roles and Expanded Influence

In 2016, Claude Edward Elkins Jr was appointed Group Vice President of Chemicals Marketing, overseeing one of the most complex and regulated freight segments. His operational discipline and attention to safety made him particularly effective in this role.

By 2018, he advanced to Vice President of Industrial Products, managing freight related to metals, construction materials, and forest products—industries vital to economic growth and infrastructure development.

Executive Leadership: Chief Commercial Officer

In December 2021, Elkins reached a career milestone when he was named Executive Vice President and Chief Commercial Officer of Norfolk Southern. In this role, he oversees intermodal, automotive, industrial products, real estate, customer logistics, and field sales.

His responsibilities require balancing customer expectations, operational realities, and long-term growth strategies in a highly competitive transportation environment.
